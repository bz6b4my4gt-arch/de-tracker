DoD-Wide Governance & FY2027 Funding

The headline change since the original chart: the FY2027 request roughly doubles DoD directed-energy RDT&E to over $2B, driven by "Golden Dome for America" and a counter-drone surge. Still no traditional DE program of record exists DoD-wide — but the Army's E-HEL is on track to become the first (production decision targeted Q4 FY2026).

Golden Dome & the Domestic "Laser Dome"

The connective tissue absent from the original charts: a homeland missile- and drone-defense push that pulls Army, Navy and Air Force DE programs into one architecture, plus an FAA safety agreement that opens the door to lasers firing over U.S. airspace.

Golden Dome for America

Trump-administration homeland missile-defense initiative. The FY2027 request puts $452M of DE RDT&E directly under Golden Dome (vs $142M enacted via the July 2025 reconciliation act). Stated goal: move DE from prototypes to operational at scale within ~3 years, with a battle-ready laser demonstration targeted for summer 2028. JLWS (counter-cruise-missile) and the BBG(X) battleship are the marquee contributions.

JIATF-401 — five-base counter-drone pilot

The Joint Interagency Task Force overseeing drone policy named five installations to receive directed-energy counter-UAS weapons (high-energy lasers + high-power microwave), operational before end of 2026:

• Fort Bliss, TX  •  Fort Huachuca, AZ  •  Grand Forks AFB, ND  •  Whiteman AFB, MO  •  Naval Base Kitsap, WA

Systems named include the Army's AMP-HEL (AeroVironment LOCUST). Funding: ~$580M JIATF-401 R&D, ~$800M potential procurement, plus $158M critical-infrastructure, $100M World Cup 2026, $20M border (FY2027). "There is no silver bullet," per Brig. Gen. Matt Ross.

FAA–Pentagon safety agreement

After March 2026 testing at White Sands (firing AMP-HEL at a grounded Boeing 767 fuselage for up to 8 seconds at max effective range with no structural damage), the FAA and Pentagon concluded laser systems "do not present an increased risk to the flying public." This follows two Feb 2026 AMP-HEL deployments near Fort Bliss / Fort Hancock that triggered temporary El Paso airspace closures. International context: France fielded HELMA-P at the 2024 Paris Olympics; China showed laser weapons at its Sept 2025 parade; the U.K. is drafting domestic laser-defense plans.
